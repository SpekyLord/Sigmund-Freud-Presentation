C.1 — The Rise of Freudian Psychology & Other Psychological Theories

Freud & the Rise of Psychology

Who Was Freud?

Sigmund Freud (1856–1939) was an Austrian neurologist and the father of psychoanalysis. He developed a scientific observational method to understand and treat the human mind, effective for neurological conditions not understood by medicine at the time.[1,2]

Id, Ego & Superego

The mind has three forces: the Id (instincts & pleasure principle), the Ego (rational mediator & reality principle), and the Superego (moral conscience & internalized standards). Mental health depends on their balance.[3,4]

Key Freudian Theories

  • The unconscious mind drives behavior below awareness
  • Dream analysis — the "royal road to the unconscious"[5]
  • Psychosexual development through five childhood stages
  • Defense mechanisms — repression, denial, projection

Other Psychological Theories

  • Carl Jung — Collective unconscious & archetypes[6]
  • Alfred Adler — Inferiority complex & social factors[7]
  • B.F. Skinner — Behaviorism; environment shapes behavior[8]
  • Abraham Maslow — Hierarchy of Needs & self-actualization[9]

Key Point: Freud's psychoanalysis was a paradigm shift — he moved the study of human behavior from philosophy and religion into science. The other theorists built on, challenged, or replaced parts of his framework.

C.2 — Controversies of Psychological Theories & Alternative Beliefs About Human Behavior

Controversies & Alternative Beliefs

Overemphasis on Sexuality

Freud argued sexual desire drives all human behavior — even in children. His psychosexual stages shocked the public and religious communities deeply.

Lack of Scientific Rigor

Karl Popper argued psychoanalysis is unfalsifiable — it can explain anything after the fact but predict nothing. Methods relied on case studies, not experiments.[10,11]

Gender & Cultural Bias

Most patients were upper-class European women. Theories like "penis envy" are considered sexist. Did not account for cultural diversity.

Religious / Spiritual Views

  • Behavior seen as a moral or spiritual matter
  • Prayer, confession, faith as forms of "treatment"
  • The soul, not the unconscious, as core identity

Indigenous Healing (PH)

  • Filipino "hilot" and "albularyo" traditions
  • Healing through rituals, herbs, community
  • Holistic: body, mind, and spirit together

Cultural / Community Views

  • "Hiya" and "utang na loob" in PH culture
  • Collectivist vs. Freud's individualist focus
  • Eastern philosophy: harmony & balance
C.3 — Effects of Human Behavior Theories & Beliefs in Society Today

Freud's Lasting Impact on Society

Mental Health Awareness

Freud normalized the idea that psychological struggles are treatable conditions — not moral failures or signs of weakness.

Talk Therapy

The idea that talking about problems can heal comes from Freud. Today's counseling and psychotherapy owe a debt to psychoanalysis.

Art & Film

Freudian concepts influenced surrealist art, novels, and movies — dream sequences and deep character psychology.

Advertising

Edward Bernays (Freud's nephew) created modern advertising by appealing to unconscious desires.[12]

Philippine Context: RA 11036

The Philippine Mental Health Act (2018) guarantees access to mental health services and integrates them into public health.[13,14]

  • Mental health long stigmatized — associated with being "baliw"
  • Only ~500 psychiatrists for 110M+ Filipinos[15]
  • Growing awareness through social media & more psych programs
  • Schools now integrating counseling services

Critical Analysis: While Freud's methods lacked scientific rigor and carried cultural bias, his core insight — that the mind deserves study and treatment — remains the foundation of modern psychology. Without Freud's paradigm shift, laws like RA 11036 may never have existed.

Summary & References

Key Takeaways & References

C.1 — The Rise

Freud pioneered psychoanalysis (id, ego, superego; unconscious mind; dream analysis). Jung, Adler, Skinner, and Maslow expanded or challenged his work.

C.2 — Controversies

Criticized for overemphasis on sexuality, unfalsifiability, and cultural bias. Alternative beliefs include religious, indigenous (hilot, albularyo), and collectivist cultural views.

C.3 — Effects

Freud's legacy lives in mental health awareness, talk therapy, art, advertising, and Philippine law (RA 11036).
